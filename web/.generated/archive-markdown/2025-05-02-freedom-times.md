On 11th January 2020, Lance Christie and 
Rowan Scott were planning an appeal to 
PBCC Leader Bruce Hales. Lance’s account 
of what happened follows:
On 11th January around 8pm, Rowan Scott 
and I were approaching 23 Trelawney Street 
Sydney in order to conduct a low key, peaceable 
appeal to Bruce Hales via video. I thought that it 
would be good to make a recorded appeal so that 
the sincerity and passion about how I felt about 
things that had happened could be exposed so 
that I could present a blueprint for change. This 
could be a good way of meeting the negativism 
and all the bad things that have been happening 
thus far.
Rowan had started the video and I was about 
to start my speech when Gareth Hales, the eldest 
son of Bruce Hales, came hurtling up from his 
next-door house – 21 Trelawney Street – and 
approached us very aggressively, shouting that 
he didn’t want this, – “no, no, no”. He put his arm 
round us both and forcibly pushed us together. 
Rowan heard a snap which later we found out to 
be a pen in my pocket which had broken with the 
force. Gareth clearly had training on how to meet 
situations like this so, by gaining control, he was 
very forcible.
There had been no introduction – no 
gentlemanly approach – he just rushed at us. 
He must have been so clear in his mind that he 
had to close us down that he forcibly wrestled 
with Rowan in an effort to get the phone off him. 
I decided that Gareth, being a strong guy, might 
well get the phone, so my instinctive reaction 
was – after having pleaded with him that we had 
a right to do what we were doing – to close him 
down. Things were getting very aggressive and 
Rowan insisted that it was an assault. Gareth 
tried to fob it off as a group hug, but that was 
clearly a lie. He was the aggressor. He was using 
force. Rowan was just hanging on to the phone 
for dear life, visibly shocked. Gareth was very 
much, very violently, intimidating us, so we had to 
stop.
I decided to side with Gareth so ordered 
Rowan to stop the recording, but Gareth kept 
up the pressure, kept up the wrestle and Rowan 
decided to keep going with the recording and the 
video. I just tried to keep out of the fray and tried 
to plead with both of them to close down.
In the way I do things, I didn’t accuse Gareth 
of being violent I tried to play the whole thing 
down by asking him not to get violent. I didn’t 
want him to turn on me. This seemed to work, 
because he seemed to calm down but then there 
was another aggressive wrestle, where I had 
to really call his name to stop but both parties 
seemed determined to persist.
In the end Rowan walked off, pursued by 
Gareth’s sons who had come out of the house, 
and I chatted to Gareth. I mentioned that I had 
been to his father’s house seven times to try and 
have a conversation with him. He just answered, 
“I know.” He said he would meet me and discuss 
everything with me on a man-to-man basis at a 
neutral location. However, when I asked for his 
phone number, he wouldn’t give me it, choosing 
rather to take the initiative to come back to me 
when he chose.
Rowan had already called the police, and they 
suggested that we went down to the Eastwood 
Police Station. I gave the police a statement and 
they said that I had to agree to come to court to 
witness the case.
